Projects

Speaker Targeting Algorithm

June 2019 - June 2020

As humans, we are easily able to focus on a specific voice when surrounded by many people who are also speaking, such as in a party. However machines are not automatically capable of this feat, and special models have to be developed to train them to be able to do so. Termed aptly as the 'Cocktail Party Problem', there have been more than one paper written on the problem, along with various principles that can be used to resolve it. As part of my Final Year Project, I partnered up with two other people to develop an algorithm that is capable of speaker separation and recognition. We developed a model based on uPIT to solve the problem of speaker separation.

  • Developed an algorithm for solving Cocktail Party Problem using RNN for speaker separation
  • Implemented deep learning principles for speaker separation and identification
  • Extracted speech averaged at 80% PESQ value of the reference audio for the separated speaker

Smart Fan

Sept 2018 - Dec 2018

In our daily life, we often leave our fan running even when we're not there, leading to wastage of electricity. To overcome this, me and my partner developed a fan that would switch on only when it detected a user within 60cm of itself. Furthermore, it would change its speed according to the distance between the user and the fan, reducing its speed as the user got closer.

  • Implemented a smart fan using a microcontroller (STM32)
  • Fan was able to detect human presence and switch on automatically
  • Fan speed based on distance between user and fan
